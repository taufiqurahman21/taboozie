No To Strangers

The instruction "Hold up your hand and say 'No' 3 times if a stranger asks you to do something you don't like!" is designed to give children a simple yet effective tool to reject unwanted requests from strangers. By raising their hand, children signal a clear, visual boundary, asserting their discomfort or refusal. Saying "No" three times gives them the time to strengthen their resolve and make a firm statement, signaling to others around them that they are uncomfortable. This approach is easy to remember and practice, helping children feel empowered in situations that may be confusing or frightening. It also encourages the development of a vital skill: the ability to say "no" firmly, which is an important first step in protecting themselves from unsafe situations.
